Stage 1 of 5

The Fly Bottle

Where have you hit the OS1 ceiling?

The Core Concept

OS1 coordination — the Belief/Reason/Observation/Agreement operating system — works through warrant-sharing. Two people coordinate when they can show each other the same reasons for believing something. Within a PRF cluster, this is smooth and powerful. Across PRF boundaries, it fails. Not because people are irrational. Because different moral architectures generate genuinely incommensurable warrants. Better arguments do not solve architectural incompatibility.

OS2 coordination asks a different question: not do we agree? but can we build what we each want by combining our capabilities, even though we have different reasons for wanting it?

"The fly is not stupid. The fly is not uninformed. The problem is that the fly is inside a structure whose nature prevents the examination itself from revealing the exit."

Describe a real situation where OS1 coordination failed for you

Think of a time when you were trying to cooperate or reach agreement with someone whose moral framework was genuinely different from yours, and better arguments — more evidence, more careful reasoning, more empathy — did not resolve it. Be specific about the situation. What were you trying to accomplish? What was the other person trying to accomplish? What did the failure look like?

Stage 2 of 5

The Constructor

What is being built, and does it have the right structure?

The Core Concept

A constructor is a system that reliably produces specific outputs from specific inputs while remaining itself unchanged and ready to produce those outputs again. The word reliably is precise: a constructor does not succeed occasionally. It succeeds consistently, because the conditions for success are encoded in its own structure.

The Green Belt Movement is a constructor. Before Wangari Maathai invented it, the inputs existed — the women, the land, the seeds. What did not exist was the system that could reliably connect them into consistent outputs. Creating that system was the invention.

Viable constructors share structural features Elinor Ostrom identified empirically: clear boundaries, protocols adapted to local conditions, monitoring, conflict resolution mechanisms, graduated accountability, and recognized legitimacy.

Identify a constructor you have encountered or want to build

This could be an institution, a community organization, a workplace team, a family system, a movement — anything that functions (or should function) as a constructor: reliably taking inputs and producing valuable outputs while remaining itself the same operating system. Describe what it is, what inputs it takes, and what outputs it produces.

Stage 3 of 5

The ACP Audit

Is this institution generative or consuming?

The Core Concept

The Agency Coherence Principle: a system meets the ACP if its operation preserves and extends the conditions under which coherent agency can be sustained across all its participants over time. A system violates the ACP if it sustains itself by consuming the coherence of its own substrate.

The three tests: All participants — not just the powerful ones. Over time — not just in the short run. Preserves and extends — not merely avoids destroying.

Name the institution you are auditing

Choose an institution you know from the inside — a workplace, a school, a community organization, a family system. You do not need to use its real name if you prefer not to.

Stage 4 of 5

Dark Coherence Detection

What looks fine but isn't?

The Core Concept

A system can appear ordered while being structurally unsustainable. Dark coherence is order without viability: visible alignment purchased through suppressed contradiction, identity drift, scar burden accumulation, and declining repair capacity. It is the state in which the system appears to have solved disagreement because disagreement no longer has safe pathways for expression.

The warning sequence: contradiction load rises first — the tension between competing demands that cannot all be satisfied simultaneously. Identity drift rises second — the gradual erosion of who you are and what you stand for. Visible coherence collapse comes last. By the time collapse is visible, the system has typically been in dark coherence for a long time.

A note on this stage: Dark coherence detection often requires recognizing patterns in situations you are still inside. If this stage becomes distressing rather than illuminating, it is fine to describe a past situation or a situation you have observed in others rather than one you are currently in. The Companion is in Recovery Mode for this stage — it will not push deeper.
Describe a dark coherence pattern you have observed

Identify a system — an institution, a relationship dynamic, a community, or a period in your own life — where surface order was hiding accumulated contradiction, identity drift, or consuming extraction. What made it look orderly from the outside? What was the inside experience? What were the warning signs that, in retrospect, appeared before the visible breakdown?

Stage 5 of 5

The Commitment

What will you build from where you stand?

The Core Concept

The Adaptive Moral Commonwealth is not a destination. It is a direction — the project of deliberately building constructors that meet the ACP at every level. The stag at civilizational scale is enormous, and no individual reaches it alone. But every OS2 constructor begins with someone who sees a shared task that multiple PRFs can each value for their own internal reasons, and who decides to invest in building the structural conditions that make it reliable.

The invitation at the end of this reading is not to agree with the argument. It is to participate in its construction from where you stand.

"We can build what we cannot agree on. And if the argument of this paper is correct, we must."

Name one shared task you will participate in building

Be specific. Not "I will be a better person" or "I will work for justice." Name a particular shared task — in your workplace, your community, your family, your field of study — that serves multiple PRF orientations simultaneously. What is the task? Who else might join it from a different PRF perspective?
