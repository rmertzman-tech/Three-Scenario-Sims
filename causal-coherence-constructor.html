Does Order Matter?

40 runs across 5 sequences, 2 topologies, 150 steps. The BCCM claim — BioBond before Civic before Institutional — is conditionally confirmed. Sequencing matters most precisely when it matters most: under fragile structural conditions. Under good topology, simultaneous deployment achieves similar stability. The finding sharpens the prescription without undermining it.

Finding 1 — Sequencing Matters Where It Matters Most

At high clustering, Standard achieves C@150=0.901 vs Reversed=0.442 — a difference that means functional stability versus marginal decline. At low clustering, the gap is even starker: Standard=0.507 vs Reversed=0.026 (near-total collapse). The wrong sequence under fragile conditions is not merely suboptimal. It is catastrophic.

BCCM Claim — Conditionally ConfirmedBioBond → Civic → Institutional produces better long-run outcomes than reversed or simultaneous deployment specifically when structural conditions are fragile (low clustering, low repair access). Under good conditions, simultaneous is slightly better on CO quality (+0.084 vs −0.042). The prescription is most critical precisely where most needed.
Finding 2 — The CO Paradox: Stability vs Coherence Orientation

Standard sequence achieves the highest C@150 but negative CO_late (−0.042). Simultaneous achieves slightly lower C (0.910) but positive CO_late (+0.084). This reveals a sequencing tradeoff: staged intervention produces more stable long-run coherence, but the Civic and Institutional phases introduced at t=30 and t=50 create temporary disruption that briefly opposes C movement, yielding negative CO in the measurement window.

Practical ImplicationIf the institutional goal is long-run stability, Standard is the prescription. If CO quality (coherence and moral expansion moving together) is the primary target, Simultaneous with adequate recovery parameters achieves it. Most real-world settings need stability first — which makes Standard the correct default, with the CO tradeoff acknowledged.
Finding 3 — Civic-First as an Intermediate Path

Civic-first (dialogue channels before recovery infrastructure) achieves C@150=0.781 at high clustering — substantially better than Reversed (0.442) but below Standard (0.901). CO_late is positive (+0.247), outperforming Standard on that dimension. This suggests: when institutions can provide repair access before individual recovery infrastructure is in place, the outcome is reasonable but not optimal. It is the pattern of well-intentioned DEI work that creates dialogue without first ensuring practitioners have adequate scar metabolization capacity.

Why the Sequence Is Load-Bearing

The reversed sequence (Institutional first) reduces conformity pressure before agents have scar recovery infrastructure. Agents are exposed to greater frame diversity while carrying maximum scar burden. The result is rapid coherence collapse — not from lack of repair access, but from excessive repair demand on agents who have no metabolization capacity.

This is the institutional analog of introducing ethics training before providing the structural conditions that make ethical engagement sustainable. The training is not wrong. The sequence is.

Practical TranslationOrganizations cannot install cross-difference dialogue programs (Civic) or challenge psychological safety norms (Institutional) before establishing the recovery infrastructure (BioBond) that allows those engagements to be metabolizable. The sequence is not a preference. It is a structural prerequisite. The simulation quantifies exactly what violating it costs.
