Finding 1 — Validation Confirmed: Information Accelerates Collapse

The Parasitic Incursions paper makes a strong and counterintuitive prediction: adding more information to a high-entropy environment does not restore agency — it accelerates collapse. The simulation confirms this in both societies. High-resilience: moderate_shock λ=0.376 → info_addition λ=0.372. Low-resilience: moderate_shock λ=0.227 → info_addition λ=0.223.

"Resistance is Thermodynamic. You cannot fight Dark Coherence with more information. You must fight it with Cooling."

The mechanism: additional information increases S_env (environmental entropy), which drives L_GC higher. The ablation term of the Master Equation grows exponentially with L_GC. At high L_GC, each additional unit of information produces more ablation than accretion — accelerating the descent of dL/dt below zero. This formalizes why fact-checking campaigns and information-flooding strategies fail against authoritarian entropy injection. The response requires reducing L_GC (stress), not increasing information volume.

Finding 2 — Cooling Is Partial, Not Curative

After the Sapolsky Gate closes (L_GC > θ_crit), cooling interventions that reduce L_GC produce only marginal λ improvement (Δλ ≈ +0.007–0.009) without reversing gate closure. The gate remains closed because the scar burden and K_drift accumulated during the gate-closed period continue to drain λ even as L_GC drops. The document specifies that once the Sapolsky Gate closes, restoring full c_N operation requires not just L_GC reduction but energy sufficient to overcome thermodynamic irreversibility (E ≥ k_B T ln 2).

BioBond Implication — Amendment No. 1 ConfirmedCooling = BioBond recovery infrastructure (r_S, r_K). The partial effectiveness finding confirms Amendment No. 1's claim: recovery from collapse requires above-standard parameters (r_S ≥ 0.25) because scar burden accumulated during collapse demands higher recovery rates than prevention-level parameters provide. The gate closes fast; metabolizing what accumulated while it was closed is the harder problem.
Finding 3 — Parasite Without Shock Is Most Dangerous

The parasite-only scenario (minimal environmental shock, high parasitic signal) produces the highest Dark Coherence rates (75%) in both societies. A parasitic signal alone — without an underlying crisis to justify it — is more effective at driving Dark Coherence than a genuine crisis without a parasitic signal. This is the mechanism the Parasitic Incursions paper describes: the parasite doesn't need to create the crisis. It needs only to amplify perceived threat beyond θ_crit. In the absence of a real shock, a sufficiently strong fear signal achieves the same result.

The Political ImplicationSocieties are not primarily at risk from catastrophes. They are at risk from agents who manufacture the epistemic equivalent of catastrophe — who inject sufficient fear and contradictory information to exceed θ_crit without any underlying environmental justification. High-resilience societies are partially protected by their higher θ_crit. Low-resilience societies close the gate even at baseline conditions.

The Critical Difference: What θ_crit Protects Against

θ_crit is not just a number — it is the accumulated institutional trust, civic capacity, and social capital that a society has built over time. High-resilience societies have a larger buffer between their baseline L_GC and their collapse threshold. Low-resilience societies are already operating close to the limit. A parasitic signal that barely moves the needle in a high-resilience society closes the gate in a low-resilience one.

The most significant implication: the parasite-only scenario closes the gate in low-resilience societies even without an underlying crisis. The parasitic signal alone — fear messaging, disinformation, manufactured outrage — is sufficient to exceed the threshold. This means the defense for low-resilience societies is not crisis management but θ_crit elevation: rebuilding institutional trust, reducing economic precarity, and restoring civic bandwidth before a parasitic actor targets the gap.

The Cooling Prescriptionθ_crit elevation is the long-run cooling intervention. In simulation terms: moving a society from θ_crit=45 to θ_crit=65 means the moderate shock scenario that closes the gate in the low-resilience society leaves it open in the high-resilience one. This is the BioBond program at civilizational scale: build institutional trust, reduce L_GC baseline, and raise the threshold before the parasitic actor arrives.
The Sapolsky Masking Function

The core equation governing cognitive capacity as a function of stress:

C_PFC(t) = C_max / (1 + exp(k × (L_GC(t) − θ_crit)))

When L_GC < θ_crit: gate open, full c_N access, democratic deliberation possible. When L_GC ≥ θ_crit: gate closes sigmoidally, c_N ablated, agent regresses to c_0 reflexes. The sigmoidal shape produces a cliff rather than a gradual decline — consistent with documented threshold effects in threat-rigidity research.

The Master Equation
dL/dt = η × C_PFC(L_GC) × Φ(I,H) − γ × S_env × exp(k × L_GC)
Accretion term: η × C_PFC × Φ — growth depends linearly on cognitive capacity. When C_PFC is high (gate open), the society can learn, integrate, and develop. When C_PFC drops (gate closing), accretion weakens.
Ablation term: γ × S_env × exp(k × L_GC) — decay grows EXPONENTIALLY with stress. This is the key nonlinearity. Each unit of additional L_GC (each additional fear signal, each additional crisis) produces more ablation than the last. This is why adding information accelerates collapse: it increases S_env, which multiplies the exponential ablation term.
Implementation Architecture — Option C Hybrid

The simulation uses the Option C hybrid architecture: the UCP V3 agent-based engine (48 agents, 4 clusters) serves as the substrate for individual coherence dynamics, while L_GC is computed as a population-level exogenous forcing variable fed back into agent state via the Sapolsky function each step.

This preserves individual agent heterogeneity (different agents respond differently to the same L_GC based on their personal C, S, and K_drift state) while modeling the population-level threshold dynamics the spec requires. The Sapolsky function scales each agent's integration value (I) before the standard V3 step runs, and the ablation multiplier increases S accumulation proportionally. Gate-closed agents additionally experience accelerated K_drift and U reduction (false certainty lock-in).
