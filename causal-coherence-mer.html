New Variables
MER — Moral Expansion Radius [0,1]. How far an agent extends moral consideration beyond their own frame. Initialized from cluster position (minority-cluster agents start higher). Expands only during genuine cross-cluster repair encounters with actual divergence. Frozen under conformity and false-narrative conditions.
ΔMER — Net MER change over the run. Positive = expanding moral consideration. Near zero = frozen. The primary output of the MER experiment.
CO — Coherence Orientation. Sign of the correlation between ΔC and ΔMER at each step. CO_avg >0 means coherence and moral expansion move together (the AMC attractor). CO_avg <0 means they work against each other — the bridge-builder paradox.

Finding 1: The Risk Is Structural, Not Content-Based

The design hypothesis was that Authority-dominant, high-Loyalty, low-Care configurations would be the primary Dark Coherence risk. The simulation contradicts this. MER freezes identically under premature consensus regardless of which frame wins — a Care-dominant network with conformity pressure is as MER-frozen as an Authority-dominant one. The mechanism is conformity, not content.

Application Implication Replace the PRF-configuration-based risk flag with the structural flag: recovery access domain ≤2 AND coherence average ≥3.0 AND relational question responses absent for three or more consecutive check-ins. This applies to Care-dominant and Authority-dominant users equally when structural conditions match.
Finding 2: The Bridge-Builder Paradox Formalized

Repair without recovery produces MER expansion (+0.13 to +0.38) while C collapses to zero. CO_avg is strongly negative. The agent is genuinely becoming more morally capacious — and losing the operational coherence needed to act from any values at all. The expansion is real. The collapse is also real. They are produced by the same mechanism: genuine cross-frame engagement without recovery infrastructure.

“The agent most at risk is not the one who has stopped examining. It is the one examining hardest without the recovery conditions that allow examination to sustain itself.”

Application Implication When coherence check-in scores are declining while Companion engagement is high and relational notes are rich — this is Type 2, not Type 1. Recovery Mode first. Do not deploy challenge exchange. The agent does not need more moral stimulation.
Finding 3: Recovery Does Not Unfreeze MER Under Conformity

In premature consensus and false narrative conditions, MER freezes within ten steps regardless of recovery parameters. Adding full recovery (r_K=0.10, r_S=0.20) to preloc or false narrative does not unfreeze MER (Δ remains ≈0.001 in all tested runs). The mechanism: MER expansion requires genuine cross-cluster repair encounters. Under conformity conditions, agents adopt the dominant frame, eliminating the frame diversity that expansion requires. Recovery restores coherence capacity. It does not restore frame diversity.

Key Structural Claim You cannot recover your way out of a network that has eliminated its own moral diversity. Frame diversity must be structurally preserved — through heterogeneous connections, through repair protocols that maintain uncertainty.
Finding 4: Class A vs Class B Stability — CO Dimension

The stability frontier (Sweep tab in causal-coherence-v4) distinguished Class A (r_S=0.20) from Class B (r_S=0.15) stability. The MER experiment adds a CO dimension that sharpens this distinction. Class A: CO_avg positive — C and MER move together. The AMC attractor. Class B: CO_avg negative — despite adequate C and expanding MER, the two dynamics are frequently decoupled.

BioBond Specification Implication Class A stability (r_S≥0.20, r_K≥0.06, high clustering) is the minimum BioBond configuration. Class B (r_S=0.15) achieves coherence stability but not CO positivity — not the AMC attractor. Both recovery parameters must be present and sufficient, not just above collapse threshold.
Next Experiments
1. Long-run confirmation (150 steps)
Run Type 4 condition to 150 steps. Does CO remain positive in long-run equilibrium, or does it eventually turn negative as scar accumulates even under recovery? The stability frontier paper confirmed C stability but not CO durability beyond 60 steps.
2. Low-clustering MER at high r_S
No low-clustering configuration achieved stability in the sweep (0/44). Run MER experiment at r_S = 0.30, 0.40 — beyond the tested range — to find whether low-clustering stability is achievable and whether it produces positive CO.
3. MER initialization sensitivity
Current initialization: minority-cluster agents start at higher MER. Does the initial MER distribution affect trajectory type assignment? Run with uniform MER initialization to test whether the cluster-based asymmetry is load-bearing.
4. Directed cross-cluster repair
Add a structural intervention: force a fraction of repair-capable edges to be cross-cluster. Does directing repair structurally change the frontier or CO positivity threshold?
