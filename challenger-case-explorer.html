The Challenger Disaster: A Case Study in Institutional Failure

On January 28, 1986, the Space Shuttle Challenger broke apart 73 seconds after launch, killing all seven crew members. This was not an accident — it was a predicted catastrophe. Engineer Roger Boisjoly had warned for months that the shuttle's design was unsafe. This case explores what happens when institutional systems destroy the people trying to protect us, and what ethics requires of you when you see what others refuse to.
