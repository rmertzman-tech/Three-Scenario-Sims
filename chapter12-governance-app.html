The central question of this chapter: The IPCC has been publishing its findings since 1988. Climate scientists have high λ Raw — substantial knowledge and analytical capacity. Yet what reaches governance decisions — λ Perceived — is a small fraction of that capacity. Why? And what does the governance architecture do to the expert and citizen populations who learn, year by year, that their signal does not change outcomes?

Procedural vs Substantive Legitimacy

▶

Procedural legitimacy is the degree to which a governance institution follows correct rules. Elections are held, laws are debated, courts follow precedent. A governance institution can have high procedural legitimacy and low routing quality simultaneously — this is captured governance: the rules are followed, but the evidence architecture is calibrated so that empirically-calibrated signal is systematically attenuated before reaching decision authority.

Routing quality is the degree to which the knowledge and interests of governed populations actually reach decisions. These two variables are independent in the simulation — and the gap between them is where the governance suppression gap lives.

The Three Configurations

▶
Config A — Captured Governance: High formal legitimacy, low routing quality. Evidence architecture weights authority-endorsed conclusions at 52% and empirical-calibrated expert input at only 10%. λ Raw is fixed at 0.72 — the expert knowledge is intact and substantial. The architecture is the filter.
Config B — Coherence-Preserving Governance: Balanced legitimacy and routing quality. Empirical-calibrated evidence weighted at 52%. Care, fairness, and community block fields are positive. λ Raw emerges naturally — not fixed — reflecting what genuine participation in coherence-preserving deliberation produces.
Config C — Reform Transition: Begins in the Config A state. A governance reform activates at step 4 — improved routing quality, reduced policy rigidity. Watch the step chart: λ Perceived rises, but morphic suppression does not immediately fall. This is the reform lag — the accumulated learned self-censorship of an expert population does not dissolve on contact with improved structural conditions.

Evidence distribution — how the governance institution weights different categories of input. Authority-weighted: defer to official hierarchy. Empirical-calibrated: weight physical evidence and expert analysis. Alternative-facts: elevated noise from advocacy distortion. Tradition-weighted: precedent and established practice.

Block fields — structural frictions and facilitators. Negative care/fairness/community means raising human consequences or distributional concerns creates institutional friction. Positive boundary means existing arrangements are strongly protected.

Institution state — policy_rigidity: how much existing arrangements resist evidence-based challenge. routing_quality: fraction of expert/citizen signal reaching decision authority. repair_bandwidth: capacity to incorporate evidence that challenges current trajectories. legitimacy: both procedural and substantive combined.

λ Raw fixed (Config A & C): Expert knowledge and citizen understanding is held constant at 0.72. The architecture is the filter — not the capacity. Config B uses emergent λ Raw, reflecting what coherence-preserving deliberation produces naturally.
