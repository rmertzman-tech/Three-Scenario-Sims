§I — The Second Filter

▶

Chapter 12 showed you the governance suppression gap: the three compounding filters — evidence distribution, block fields, routing quality — that determine how much of the available knowledge reaches decision authority. Chapter 13 introduces a prior question. Before any of those governance filters operate, there is a second set of filters that determines what information is produced, distributed, and weighted in the first place.

Information systems — the architectures through which knowledge is generated, communicated, amplified, and suppressed — are the filters upstream of the filters Chapter 12 measured. If governance has low routing quality for empirical-calibrated signal, the problem is severe. If the information systems feeding governance simultaneously degrade the quality and coherence of that signal before it arrives, the problem is multiplicative.

The Ch13 distinction from Ch12. Chapter 12 modeled the filtering of intact expert capacity — λ Raw was high (Challenger: 0.72) and the governance architecture filtered it. Chapter 13 models what happens when the information architecture degrades what agents can generate. In Config A, λ Raw is 0.107 — not because human capacity is absent, but because the information environment does not sustain the cognitive conditions for deliberate, coherent contribution. The architecture changes what gets generated, not only what gets received.

§II — A Labour of Harm: The CSER Research

▶

This chapter deploys research from the Centre for the Study of Existential Risk at Cambridge: Ghionis, Connell, Hobson, Armitage, and Sundaram's "A Labour of Harm: Artificial Intelligence and Biological Weapons Acquisition" (2025). The paper's core finding and the EBB framework's core finding are the same argument in different vocabularies.

The existing AI-biosecurity literature concentrates on laboratory-facing capabilities — can AI design a pathogen, accelerate experiments, lower technical barriers? The paper argues this compresses biological weapons acquisition into a narrow set of technical moments, abstracting away the organizational processes through which harmful capability is historically built, sustained, and — most importantly — abandoned.

Web of Acquisition

A seven-stage scaffold: Inspiration → Planning → Development → Manufacture/Production → Storage/Transportation → Targeting → Dissemination. The web compels analysis to consider the full set of practical challenges historically shaping the feasibility of harmful efforts.

Labour of Harm

The situated, evolving, often improvised organizational work occurring within and across those stages: technical, organizational, cognitive, and logistical. Shaped by friction, failure, and recalibration. The decisive variable determining whether acquisition efforts succeed, stall, or are abandoned.

The organizational coherence bottleneck. The paper's most important contribution: most historical biological weapons programs failed not for technical reasons but organizational ones. Assembling and maintaining a team, managing secrecy, absorbing failure without abandoning the program, interpreting uncertainty, sustaining commitment — these are the decisive constraints. In the EBB framework: organizational coherence is exactly what the information architecture produces or destroys. The labour of harm concept is the suppression gap applied to organizational processes rather than governance decisions.

§III — The Four Traditions at the Scale of Information Systems

▶

Each ethical tradition has something correct to say about AI and information ethics — and as in every previous chapter, each individual-level reading is insufficient. The OS2 column below adds the structural causal claim.

Virtue Ethics — Who are you becoming? click to expand ▾
OS1 — Individual Reading

AI developers, platform operators, and users all face character questions. The proliferation of AI-generated misinformation reflects failures of practical wisdom across an entire industry. Better character in key decision-makers would produce better information environments.

OS2 — Structural Extension

What information architecture produces or destroys the conditions under which practical wisdom can be exercised at scale? Config A's 30% alternative-facts elevation and negative community block fields do not merely tempt users toward vice — they degrade the cognitive conditions under which deliberate ethical reasoning is possible, shrinking the population operating in Manual Mode.

Deontology — What is your duty? click to expand ▾
OS1 — Individual Reading

AI developers have a Kantian duty not to create systems that treat users as means to engagement metrics rather than rational ends. Deceptive design, addictive algorithms, and misinformation amplification all violate the categorical imperative.

OS2 — Structural Extension

What information architecture enables rational agency — the capacity to reason, deliberate, choose autonomously — and what architecture systematically degrades it? An authority-weighted, alternative-facts-elevated environment performs the structural Kantian violation: it treats the entire population's rational agency as an instrumental resource for attention capture, degrading the conditions that make the categorical imperative applicable.

Consequentialism — What does this produce? click to expand ▾
OS1 — Individual Reading

The aggregate harms of AI-enabled misinformation, algorithmic radicalization, and attention exploitation are enormous and measurable. Better-designed AI systems could produce better aggregate outcomes. The expected value calculation clearly favors intervention.

OS2 — Structural Extension

What does this architecture produce as a population-level emergent pattern across millions of interactions? Config A produces OS2 Bridge at 0.314 — cross-worldview coordination 42% lower than Config B's 0.540. The aggregate harm is not individual misinformation encounters but the systematic degradation of the population's capacity to coordinate across worldview differences — the capacity democratic deliberation requires.

Care Ethics — Who are you in relation with? click to expand ▾
OS1 — Individual Reading

AI systems that amplify outrage, accelerate polarization, and sever relational connections between communities fail their relational obligations to the users they affect and to the communities those users inhabit.

OS2 — Structural Extension

What information architecture severs or sustains the relational networks through which different communities can understand each other? Config A's community block field (−0.45) represents an environment that treats community-oriented, relational contributions as noise. The care ethics OS2 extension asks what this does to the social substrate of deliberation — not individual relationships but the institutional conditions under which relationships across difference can be sustained.

The same note as every previous traditions table: the OS1 column is correct and insufficient. Individual AI developers making better choices, platform operators designing more responsibly, users exercising greater critical judgment — all matter and none is sufficient. The information architecture is the primary causal agent.

§V — The Horizon of This Instrument

▶

The simulation models one information system institution. The actual AI-information landscape involves multiple institutional actors: AI developers, platform operators, regulatory agencies, civil society accountability organizations, and media institutions — each with its own institutional state and connected through trust vectors that have been building or decaying since the internet became the primary information infrastructure.

The research extension forward pointer. Chapter 14's multi-institutional trust vector extension applies directly here. The trust vector between AI developers and regulatory agencies is currently at very low Γ: large social distance between technical expertise concentrated in developer institutions and regulatory authority concentrated in agencies without equivalent technical capacity. The AI governance field application of the multi-institutional simulation follows directly from Chapter 14's framework.

Evidence distribution — the population-level weighting of four epistemic processing modes. Authority-weighted (48% in Config A): agents defer to official hierarchy. Empirical-calibrated (8% in Config A, 58% in Config B): agents update from observable data. Alternative-facts (30% in Config A): agents actively reinterpret evidence to match prior beliefs — the AI-generated noise channel. Tradition-weighted: precedent and established practice.

Block fields — structural amplifiers and penalties on how contributions are framed. Config A: care (−0.42), fairness (−0.38), community (−0.45) all negative — human consequences, distributional concerns, and collective interests create friction. Config B: all three positive — these framings advance deliberation. Boundary: +0.35 in Config A (status quo strongly amplified).

Institution state — entropy_export 0.70 in Config A (highest in any chapter simulation) models an information system that generates disorder and routes it downward onto agents. Routing_quality 0.25: only 25% of what emerges from deliberation reaches decision authority. Repair_bandwidth 0.15: minimal capacity to incorporate evidence that challenges current information trajectories.

The Ch13 finding: the information architecture's three filters — evidence distribution, block fields, institution state — compound to degrade what agents generate (λ Raw = 0.107 in Config A) not only what they transmit. The suppression gap between λ Raw and λ Perceived is tiny (0.007) because both are low. The architecture shapes what is produced, not only what is received.
