The T1/T2 distinction is the most practically important tool in this chapter. Understanding which type an action is determines whether it can close the coordination gap.

Type 1 — Structural

Changes ε, φ, ρ, σ, or μ directly. Raises FHRI_max ceiling. Durable because structural capacity to coordinate actually changes. Examples: ownership type change, staffing mandate, ownership disclosure requirement, BioBond instrument.

Type 2 — Agent/Process

Improves agents, incentives, or processes within unchanged structural parameters. Cannot exceed FHRI_max. May produce temporary improvement. Examples: VBP payment adjustments, training programs, professional ethics codes, internal advocacy within existing institutional rules.

The trap. T2 interventions matter within the ceiling they operate in. The trap is substituting T2 for T1 when the ceiling itself is the problem. HHVBP is a T2 intervention producing +0.30% average adjustment where ε = 0.636 requires T1 structural change.
