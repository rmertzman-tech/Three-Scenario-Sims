The World Values Map

Ronald Inglehart and Christian Welzel analyzed values data from over 90 countries and 400,000+ respondents across four decades. Their World Values Survey identified two dimensions that account for most of the variation in human values across societies. The map below shows approximate cultural cluster positions. Hover over any cluster to see how it maps onto the simulation's worldview parameters.

Reading the Map in Simulation Terms

The horizontal axis (Survival → Self-Expression) maps directly to the base_stress and base_entropy parameters in the simulation. Moving left on this axis is equivalent to raising base_stress above 0.55. The vertical axis (Traditional → Secular-Rational) maps to the evidence_distribution parameter — Traditional authority correlates with authority_weighted processing; Secular-Rational correlates with empirical_calibrated. Every simulation run you have done was placing agents somewhere on this map.

The Two Axes

The map is built from two independent dimensions. Knowing a culture's position on one tells you nothing reliable about the other. Together they describe the two most fundamental variables in how human values actually distribute across societies.

↕ Vertical Axis — Source of Authority
Traditional

Authority derives from inherited sources: religious texts, ancestral practice, hierarchical roles, sacred identity. What is right was established before the current generation and is not subject to revision by individual reasoning.

Secular–Rational

Authority derives from process: empirical evidence, logical argument, bureaucratic legitimacy, democratic deliberation. What is right can in principle be revised if evidence or argument warrants it.

Simulation link: evidence_distribution parameter. Traditional → authority_weighted & tradition_weighted. Secular-Rational → empirical_calibrated. This axis is the dominant predictor of suppression gap.
↔ Horizontal Axis — Thermodynamic State
Survival

Generated by material insecurity: poverty, physical threat, institutional instability. Tribalism is thermodynamically efficient — trust the known, exclude the unknown. Short time horizons forced by immediate threats.

Self–Expression

Generated by material surplus and security. Tolerance is affordable when failure is survivable. Diversity becomes an asset. Long time horizons become available because the present isn't consuming all available energy.

Simulation link: base_stress & base_entropy. Survival → high base_stress (0.55–0.75), negative block fields. Self-Expression → low base_stress (0.20–0.40), positive blocks. This axis is the dominant predictor of λ Raw.

The Critical Insight

These are not fixed identities — they are thermodynamic positions. The same population can move on this map over decades as material conditions change. The Inglehart-Welzel data shows this directly: as economic security arrives, populations move toward Self-Expression values on their own, not because they were instructed to. And under genuine survival pressure, populations move back toward Survival values, not from moral failure but from rational thermodynamic response.

The Retroactive Payoff

Every simulation run from Chapter 2 through Chapter 8 was, without your knowing it, modeling a specific position or trajectory on the Inglehart-Welzel map. The Challenger case was a role_order / authority_weighted configuration — Traditional/Survival institutional architecture in a high-stress decision environment. The TRC was a deliberate attempt to build Secular-Rational / Self-Expression architecture in a population that had been forced by decades of apartheid into Survival/Traditional positions. The map was always there. Now you can read it directly.

indigenous_custodial under stress — why it's different from role_order

Both are Traditional in authority, but they respond to stress differently. Role_order agents under stress intensify hierarchy — authority escalates, deviants are suppressed. Indigenous_custodial agents under stress contract toward community protection — the group closes ranks, but the orientation remains custodial rather than hierarchical. In block_fields: role_order generates negative community blocks when stressed; indigenous_custodial maintains positive community blocks longer before they turn. This difference is visible in the simulation and corresponds to real cross-cultural variation in how communities respond to economic collapse.

The Thermodynamic Logic of Values

The single most important insight the map provides — and the one most consistently misread in political and ethical debate — is this: position on the Survival/Self-Expression axis is not primarily a moral or intellectual achievement. It is a thermodynamic response to material conditions.

Why Self-Expression Values Are Expensive

Extending genuine trust to strangers requires metabolic resources that can only be allocated when survival needs are already met. Tolerating difference requires enough surplus to absorb the coordination cost of diversity without it becoming a threat. Caring about the environment, about future generations, about people outside your immediate community — these require a future horizon, which requires a present that is not consuming all available energy defending against immediate threats.

The Sapolsky Threshold at Cultural Scale

In Chapter 1, you learned that chronic stress pushes the Prefrontal Cortex offline and forces the agent into reactive, binary processing. The Inglehart-Welzel X-axis is that same mechanism operating at the level of entire populations across generations.

A society that has lived with sustained poverty, physical insecurity, or institutional instability has had its base_stress parameter set high for decades or centuries. The value distributions that result — tribalism, rigid conformity, short time horizons, low tolerance — are not moral failures. They are the culturally encoded survival strategies of populations whose Sapolsky threshold has been chronically exceeded.

The map does not tell you what people should value. It tells you what conditions produce which values. Changing the values without changing the conditions is the thermodynamic equivalent of asking someone to run when their legs are broken.

WVM Position as IPS Output

The V5.0 engine makes a structural connection that the WVM alone cannot show: a population’s position on the Survival/Self-Expression axis is partly determined by the IPS profile of the institutions governing it. When the institutions that organize economic life, healthcare, and public safety have high ε (extraction rate) and high φ (physical entropy), they impose the biological conditions for Survival-values territory. FHRI_max is low — not because residents lack the capacity for Self-Expression values, but because the structural ceiling the institutions impose keeps the metabolic conditions for those values structurally unavailable.

The Florida nursing home case is a small-scale demonstration of a large-scale principle: PE-acquired facilities (FHRI_max = 13.6) produce staff and resident populations operating under conditions that anchor them toward Survival/Traditional values — short time horizons, high threat sensitivity, low trust extension. Non-profit facilities (FHRI_max = 55.1) produce conditions consistent with movement toward Self-Expression. Same regulatory environment, same workforce market, same patient population. The IPS profile of the institution is the causal mechanism the WVM position reflects at scale.

The AMC forward pointer: The reason BioBond instruments target FHRI_max improvement is precisely because FHRI_max improvement is the mechanism through which populations gain the metabolic conditions to move on the World Values Map. This is empirically testable: the cross-national IPS profiles of countries should predict their WVM positions better than GDP alone.

The Implication for Policy

You cannot export Self-Expression institutional architecture to Survival environments by argument alone. Every colonial and development intervention that failed — and most of them did — failed in part because it tried to install Self-Expression software on Survival hardware. The simulation models this precisely: you cannot maintain positive block_fields at high base_stress. The architecture collapses not from bad values but from insufficient thermodynamic conditions to sustain those values institutionally.

The reverse trajectory is where the map's most important empirical finding lies: when material conditions stabilize, populations move toward Self-Expression values on their own — not because they were instructed to, but because the metabolic budget became available to sustain the institutional architecture that Self-Expression values require.
