What Is Capability-Based Coordination?

This model suggests that successful coordination doesn't require everyone to agree on values. Instead, it emerges when different groups contribute their unique abilities toward a shared task.

Personal Reality Frameworks (PRFs): Each stakeholder sees the world through a unique lens — shaped by beliefs, rules, and values. In this simulation, you'll see the PRF behind every choice.

Functional Equivalence: Different actions can be equivalent because they advance the same coordinated task. A scientist publishing a paper and a family member giving testimony are functionally equivalent — both contribute to establishing the truth about CTE, even though they're driven by completely different values.

Bootstrap Resolution: Authority is built through demonstrated capability. When a group asks a powerful, evidence-based question, they gain legitimate authority even if they started with no formal power. This is OS2 thinking in action — coordination across PRF difference rather than requiring PRF agreement.

    What Do These Numbers Actually Measure?

    Each metric has a felt, human meaning — not just a score. These are not performance ratings. They describe the structural conditions inside which coordination is or isn't possible for the people in this crisis.

    λ Raw

    Coordination Capacity — the upper ceiling of what this network could achieve

    λ Raw is not a measure of effort, goodwill, or motivation. It is a measure of cognitive access — whether the kind of thinking required for careful, principled coordination is structurally available to the people in this network. In the CTE crisis, λ Raw degrades when institutional pressure consumes the cognitive bandwidth that would otherwise go toward deliberate, reflective action.

    High — > 0.70
    Manual mode. Clear thinking available.
    Dr. Omalu can hold the full complexity of the evidence, the institutional pressure, and his professional obligations simultaneously — and act on all three. The scientist, the family, the journalist can coordinate deliberately.
    Degrading — 0.40–0.70
    Still possible but it costs more.
    The family member knows something is wrong but keeps second-guessing herself. The journalist has the story but keeps pulling punches. The capacity is there — the institutional drag is consuming it before it reaches action.
    Collapsed — < 0.40
    Survival mode. Principled coordination unavailable.
    The athlete isn't thinking about the long-term health problem anymore. He's thinking about whether raising it is safe. The infrastructure for deliberate ethical reasoning has been spent on self-protection.
    🔑 In the CTE context
    High λ Raw does not mean everyone agrees or that coordination is easy. It means the conditions under which deliberate, principled action is possible are intact. Low λ Raw means those conditions have been structurally consumed — by contract pressure, career risk, institutional gaslighting — regardless of anyone's intentions.

    λ Perceived

    Experienced Coordination — what people actually feel they can safely do

    λ Raw asks: what could this network do? λ Perceived asks: what does it feel safe to actually do? The gap between the two is the most ethically significant number in the simulation. It is the Boisjoly number — high capacity, near-zero perceived ability to surface it. In the CTE crisis, every stakeholder who self-censors, softens their story, or "decides not to make it a big deal" is living inside that gap.

    High — > 0.70
    Voice is reaching decisions.
    When the scientist testifies and the family speaks publicly and the journalist publishes — and it changes something. What people know is reaching the people who need to act on it. The room is using its full intelligence.
    Constrained — 0.40–0.70
    Participation requires constant calibration.
    The athlete mentions his headaches but not his memory loss. The family member speaks to one journalist but not another. You're still in the conversation — but a version of you that has already filtered itself. Some of what you know is circulating in the hallway after the meeting.
    Blocked — < 0.40
    Presence without voice. Effectively mute.
    The family knows exactly what happened. The scientist has the data. But the structural conditions make surfacing it genuinely dangerous. They are physically present and functionally absent. This is what the NFL's suppression campaign was designed to produce.
    🔑 The suppression gap
    λ Raw − λ Perceived = the gap. In the CTE crisis this gap was not produced by explicit silencing alone — it emerged from accumulated institutional learning. Stakeholders correctly understood what the NFL's system rewarded and punished, and adjusted accordingly. The structure did the work silently.

    Friction

    Coordination Cost — energy consumed before any work reaches the problem

    Friction is not the same as suppression. Suppression is about voice — what can safely be said. Friction is about drag — how much energy the coordination process itself consumes before any of it reaches the actual problem. In the CTE network, friction comes from the genuine worldview distance between stakeholders: the scientist and the family member and the journalist are trying to coordinate across radically different frameworks for what counts as evidence, what counts as authority, and what counts as a legitimate claim.

    Low — < 0.25
    Energy reaches the problem directly.
    The distributed network has found its shared language. The scientist's data, the family's testimony, and the journalist's investigation are reinforcing each other without requiring constant translation. This is OS2 coordination functioning.
    Moderate-High — 0.25–0.65
    Coordination costs more than it should.
    The scientist and the family member are trying to work together, but their frameworks for what's important keep colliding. Every meeting requires translating between "the p-value is 0.003" and "my husband was a different person." Both are true. Getting them to reinforce each other takes work.
    Severe — > 0.65
    Most energy consumed before work begins.
    The network is spending more energy navigating its internal differences than it is applying to the NFL. Each stakeholder is starting to wonder whether the coalition is worth the cost. The NFL doesn't need to suppress anything — the friction is doing it for them.
    🔑 Friction vs suppression — why the distinction matters
    Suppression is produced by power asymmetry — fixing it requires changing power dynamics. Friction is produced by worldview distance — fixing it requires better coordination scaffolding: shared protocols, structured dialogue, OS2 bridge-building. BioBond in the simulation reduces both, but through different mechanisms. Treating them as the same problem leads to interventions that fail.

    OS2 Synergy

    Cross-PRF Bridge — the app's central concept made visible

    OS2 Synergy is the metric most specific to what this app is teaching. It measures whether agents with different worldviews are successfully using their distinct capabilities to advance a shared task — without needing to agree on why the task matters. This is capability-based coordination. This is the CTE network at its best: a scientist who believes in empirical truth, a family who believes in justice, a journalist who believes in accountability, and an athlete who believes in his teammates — all advancing the same goal through completely different PRF lenses.

    High — > 0.70
    Capability bridge functioning.
    The network is doing something remarkable: people who disagree about everything are producing the same outcome together. The scientist doesn't need to share the family's grief. The family doesn't need to understand the p-value. Each contributes their capability; the task advances. This is OS2 coordination.
    Partial — 0.40–0.70
    Some cross-PRF bridging occurring.
    Some stakeholders are coordinating across worldview difference effectively. Others are still siloed. The network is working, but it's leaving capability on the table — some contributions that could reinforce others aren't connecting yet.
    Absent — < 0.40
    No effective cross-PRF coordination.
    Each stakeholder is doing their own thing for their own reasons, but the contributions aren't building on each other. The scientist publishes; the family grieves; the journalist reports — but they're not yet a network. The NFL doesn't need to fight them. They're not yet fighting together.
    🔑 This is why PRF agreement is not required
    High OS2 Synergy is the engine's confirmation of the app's core claim: coordination does not require shared values. It requires shared task and compatible capabilities. The CTE network eventually forced a $1 billion settlement not because everyone agreed on why player safety matters — but because enough different stakeholders contributed their distinct capabilities to the same coordinated goal that the NFL could no longer ignore any of them.
