The Agency Conditions Principle

Every ethical framework — consequentialism, duty ethics, virtue ethics, care ethics, contractarianism — implicitly assumes something it almost never states explicitly: that the agents whose actions it evaluates are functioning moral agents. Capable of deliberation. Able to perceive their situation accurately. Possessing the temporal horizon required to connect present action to future consequence.

These are not descriptions of ideal humans. They are enabling conditions — the structural prerequisites without which moral concepts become empty. You cannot be responsible for an action if you lacked the cognitive coherence to understand it. You cannot keep a promise if your institutional context has eroded the temporal stability that promise-keeping requires.

Because coherent, temporally extended, reason-responsive agency is the necessary
enabling condition for responsibility, promise-keeping, care, and common life,
there are strong moral reasons to preserve the conditions
that make such agency possible.

When institutions systematically destroy those conditions,
they commit a structural injustice —
even when no single actor intended the harm.

Three features that distinguish the ACP

1
Enabling conditions, not outcomes
Standard ethical analysis asks: did this institution produce bad outcomes? The ACP asks: did it systematically degrade the conditions under which agents can function as moral actors at all? An institution can produce acceptable visible outcomes while quietly consuming the coordination capacity that would be needed to recognize and resist its extraction architecture.
2
Structural, not dispositional
The ACP locates the moral wrong in the ownership architecture that achieved P-adic proximity and executed Interpretant replacement — not in any individual actor’s bad intentions. The nurse at FHRI_max = 13.6 is not the responsible party. The PE ownership architecture that produced FHRI_max = 13.6 is the formally derived responsible party.
3
Cross-PRF accessible
Because the ACP is grounded in enabling conditions rather than any specific moral tradition’s account of the good, it is accessible from all OS1 frameworks simultaneously. Every PRF cluster presupposes functioning moral agency. The ACP formalizes what all of them require but none of them states.

What the ACP does to professional ethics

STANDARD PROFESSIONAL ETHICS
Individual practitioners have obligations defined by professional codes. When structural conditions make those obligations difficult, practitioners should exercise greater personal virtue, advocate more loudly, and if necessary leave the profession.
ACP-GROUNDED PROFESSIONAL ETHICS
The codes presuppose structural conditions that may not exist. When FHRI_max falls below the threshold at which the code’s demands are achievable, moral responsibility for the gap is primarily the ownership architecture’s. The practitioner’s obligations shift: refuse the most serious ACP violations, document structural conditions preventing full compliance, and take T1 structural action where available.

Your Professional Obligations Under the ACP

The ACP produces three specific professional ethics obligations that standard codes do not fully articulate. They are not demands for heroism. They are the minimum required for genuine professional ethics practice in institutions with measurable structural ceilings.
OBLIGATION 1 — PERCEPTION BEFORE COMPLIANCE
Before asking “how do I comply with my professional code in this institution,” ask “what is this institution’s structural capacity for the professional practice the code demands?” A practitioner who cannot measure FHRI_max formally can still ask the five IPS questions qualitatively: What fraction of my coordination capacity reaches my clients? What is the debt-to-value ratio this institution imposes on the people I serve? How misaligned are my employer’s incentives with the outcomes my professional code requires?
OBLIGATION 2 — STRUCTURAL ADDRESS OVER CHARACTER ADDRESS
When professional ethics failures occur, the first question the ACP requires is: what is the FHRI_max ceiling this practitioner was operating under? Character assessment is warranted only after structural assessment. The correctional officer who did not file a safety report may have failed in their professional duty, or may have been operating at FHRI_max = 10.9 where the organizational immune response to safety reports is absorbed to near-zero. These are very different ethical situations requiring very different responses.
OBLIGATION 3 — T1 ACTION AS PROFESSIONAL OBLIGATION
When practitioners identify T1 structural conditions systematically preventing the code’s demands from being achievable, they have an obligation to take T1-level structural action. This is not the same as heroism. It is the structural version of the existing professional obligation to “report concerns through appropriate channels” — specifying what “appropriate channels” means at the structural level: channels that can address the P-adic access condition, not merely report the symptom.

The AMC Architecture: Four Defining Features

The Adaptive Moral Commonwealth is not a political program. It is a constructor-oriented political economy: an institutional architecture that redirects task-based efficiency toward preservation and repair of the conditions under which coherent moral agency can function. It is an OS2 design because it is grounded in enabling conditions rather than any specific moral tradition’s account of the good — and therefore accessible from all OS1 frameworks simultaneously.

Click each feature to expand its full description.

1
Measurement Before Prescription
FHRI_max trajectory as the primary institutional evaluation criterion
The AMC requires that institutions be evaluated on FHRI_max trajectory — whether they are increasing or decreasing the structural coordination capacity available to the people they serve — before any specific policy intervention is prescribed. This is the OS2 foundation: agreement on what to measure before agreement on what to do. The four sector analyses are the first-pass calibration of this measurement across sectors. The measurement is not ideology-specific: a conservative institution that maintains FHRI_max above 60 and a progressive institution that does the same are both AMC-compatible regardless of their differences at the OS1 level.

In practice: Every major institutional decision — acquisition, restructuring, regulatory reform, contract renewal — requires a FHRI_max impact assessment as a precondition. This is not a new bureaucratic layer. It is the institutional equivalent of environmental impact assessment: a structural measurement that makes visible what is currently invisible in standard accounting.
2
True Solvency Accounting
φ imposed on communities appears as a liability on the imposing institution’s balance sheet
Standard accounting measures what appears in market prices. True Solvency Accounting requires that φ imposed on communities, workers, and public systems appear as a liability on the imposing firm’s balance sheet, measured by the FHRI_max degradation the firm produces in the systems it affects.

Why this matters: A PE-acquired nursing home that appears profitable because it has externalized $49 FHRI_max points of degradation onto residents, families, and communities is not actually solvent. It has borrowed against a balance sheet belonging to others. A for-profit university that appears profitable because it has externalized $42,000 in debt against $35,200 in earnings onto its graduates is running a hidden deficit on the balance sheet of those graduates.

The mechanism: True Solvency Accounting extends existing accounting standards for environmental remediation and product liability to FHRI_max degradation. Institutions that impose FHRI_max degradation on communities carry that degradation as a balance sheet liability audited annually. Institutions that restore FHRI_max carry restoration as a verifiable asset. Capital markets can then distinguish genuinely profitable institutions from institutions that appear profitable because their accounting does not capture the full cost of their operations.
3
BioBond Instruments
Making FHRI_max improvement financially rational rather than relying on institutional virtue
BioBond financial instruments make FHRI_max improvement financially rational without requiring the extraction architecture’s practitioners to adopt the values of any particular OS1 framework. The margin ratchet structure changes the financial incentives of the extraction architecture from within the market mechanism rather than imposing a regulatory override.

The mechanism: BioBond instruments carry interest rates indexed to FHRI_max. At FHRI_max < 15 (PE nursing home range), the institution pays base rate + 3%. At FHRI_max 60+ (mission-aligned range), the institution pays base rate − 2.5%. Over a 10-year bond horizon, the cumulative financial advantage of operating at FHRI_max 60+ versus FHRI_max 13.6 is large enough to make the extraction architecture more expensive than the coordination-capacity architecture — for the first time in most of these sectors’ histories.

The Maathai principle: The BioBond is not a government subsidy to good institutions. It is a mechanism that makes the good institutions’ structural advantages financially legible in capital markets that currently cannot distinguish them from extraction architectures.
4
The Sequencing Logic: BioBond → Civic → Institutional
Repair must follow biological reality, not administrative convenience
The AMC does not impose institutional reform on populations whose biological capacity for engaging with reform has been degraded below the threshold at which engagement is neurologically possible. The sequencing logic — BioBond first, then Civic participation, then Institutional redesign — reflects a biological constraint, not a political preference.

BioBond first: Restore physical safety, metabolic security, and predictable social environment. Without this, the prefrontal cortex remains in chronic stress response and cannot perform the deliberation that civic participation and institutional redesign require.

Civic second: Once biological capacity is restored above the threshold (λ > 0.4), genuine participation in governance becomes possible. Not before. Civic engagement attempted while populations remain biologically degraded produces conflict, not coordination.

Institutional third: Only after civic capacity is established (λ > 0.6) can institutional redesign succeed. Institutional reform without civic legitimacy produces backlash. Institutional reform without biological foundation produces reform that the population cannot sustain.

The implication: A nurse at FHRI_max = 13.6 cannot be asked to engage with institutional reform processes as their first intervention. Their first intervention is restoring enough FHRI_max that the institutional reform process is neurologically accessible.

Cross-PRF Commons: How Every OS1 Framework Endorses the AMC

The AMC does not require that you abandon your OS1 moral commitments. It requires only that you acknowledge what your OS1 framework already presupposes: that functioning moral agency is a precondition for whatever moral goods you value. Every major OS1 framework independently endorses the AMC from within its own values — not because they all agree, but because they all depend on the same structural enabling conditions.
🤝
Care & Fairness
Justice requires that the φ of Interpretant replacement fall on those who executed the replacement, not on those who depended on the institution. The AMC’s True Solvency Accounting mechanism routes φ back to its source through balance sheet liability. BioBond financing for CDFIs and low-ε institutions expands the structural capacity for genuine care in underserved communities. The Maathai sequencing protects against repair efforts that harm the populations they aim to serve by demanding biological restoration before civic engagement.
⚖
Liberty & Markets
Genuine market competition requires exit options and accurate price signals. Both are structurally unavailable when μ = 0.84 and the exit option costs more than the asset being defended. The AMC’s FHRI_max measurement provides the information signal that markets need but cannot generate internally when φ is off the balance sheet. True Solvency Accounting levels the competitive playing field between extraction architectures that appear profitable through externalization and genuine-value-creation architectures that bear their full costs. The AMC is pro-market in the deepest sense: it makes market mechanisms work correctly by making externalized φ visible.
🏭
Authority & Loyalty
Stable institutions, reliable professional standards, and the intergenerational transmission of civic norms all require that the institutional fabric maintain FHRI_max above the threshold at which those norms can be practiced and transmitted. Institutions with FHRI_max = 10.9 (GEO Group) or 13.6 (PE nursing homes) cannot transmit professional norms because their structural ceiling prevents professional standards from being practiced. The AMC restores the institutional conditions that make norm transmission possible — which is the deepest requirement of the authority/loyalty framework.
✨
Sanctity & Community
The sacred duty of care — for the elderly, the incarcerated, the student, the borrower — has been made structurally inaccessible by the ownership architectures the four sector analyses document. The faith community’s institutions (non-profit nursing homes at FHRI_max = 62.9, CDFIs at 58–65, mission-aligned community colleges at 58–64) consistently produce higher FHRI_max than their for-profit counterparts. The AMC provides the institutional architecture that makes the faith community’s structural advantage financially competitive at scale. The sacred obligation does not change; the structural conditions for fulfilling it become accessible.
