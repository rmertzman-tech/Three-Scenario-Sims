ε = 0.71 — EXTRACTION RATE (peak 2007)
CDO leverage reached 30–40x, meaning one dollar of underlying asset supported 30–40 dollars of instrument value — extracting yield while externalizing risk. The originate-to-distribute model removed all incentive for creditworthiness assessment: the originating bank sold the mortgage immediately and bore no default risk. ε measures how completely the goal-function “assess creditworthiness” was replaced by “maximize origination volume.”

Pre-crisis: regulatory architecture produced compliance theater without addressing structural risk. Post-crisis: Dodd-Frank created 2,300 pages of regulations and ~$36B in annual compliance costs — but did not restore the Glass-Steagall wall. ρ measures channel congestion that produces compliance activity without structural change: the T2 trap in regulatory form.
σ = 0.78 — MORPHIC RESIDUE (rating agency normalization)

Rating agencies were paid per rating issued by the entity being rated. At every point in the securitization chain the financial reward was for volume and complexity rather than creditworthiness. μ = 0.84 means the incentive architecture was 84% misaligned with genuine financial intermediation.

Recovery: T1 vs T2 — The Structural Difference

The T1/T2 distinction is formally derivable from the P-SAGE analysis. T1 interventions address the P-adic access condition — they change who is inside the cluster controlling the Signal channel or what that cluster is legally permitted to do. T2 interventions operate within the existing Interpretant replacement. The 2008 recovery is the most extensively documented T2 response to a T1 structural failure in modern economic history.
T2 Response: What Was Actually Done
TARP ($700B bailout): T2. Made the actors who achieved P-adic proximity whole. Did not change the access condition. The ownership architecture that produced FHRI_max = 11.2 continued operating with new capital.

Dodd-Frank Act (2010): T2 with partial T1 elements. 2,300 pages of compliance requirements (ρ rising further). The Volcker Rule was a genuine T1 attempt — prohibiting proprietary trading with depositor funds — but implemented with 388 pages of exceptions that preserved the access condition it nominally addressed.

Quantitative Easing: T2. Restored liquidity within the unchanged ownership architecture. FHRI_max partially restored to ~28 by 2012 — sufficient for the financial system to resume operation, insufficient to restore the populations bearing φ.

Result: Financial system FHRI_max stalled at 28–32. Household φ unaddressed for nearly a decade. Structural conditions for recurrence preserved intact.
T1 Counterfactual: Structural Reform
Glass-Steagall equivalent: True T1. Remove investment banks from the P-adic cluster controlling retail mortgage origination and credit risk assessment. Separate entities permitted to originate consumer credit from entities permitted to securitize and trade it. This changes who is inside the channel — not what the channel produces while the same actors remain inside it.

Rating agency restructuring: True T1. A structure where credit ratings are produced by entities that do not receive payment from the entities being rated changes the P-adic access condition for Interpretant assignment. The issuer-pays model is the specific instrument that placed rating agencies inside the extraction architecture.

Originate-to-hold requirements: True T1. Requiring originators to retain a minimum fraction of originated loans changes μ structurally: incentives now include long-term loan performance, not just origination volume.

Projected FHRI_max under T1: 42–48 within 5 years — functional state. Structural conditions for recurrence resolved rather than preserved.
The formally predicted consequence of T2-only recovery: the P-adic access condition is unchanged. The SVB and Signature Bank failures (2023) demonstrate this precisely: the T2 recovery from 2008 preserved the access conditions for a subsequent crisis in a new form. The S&L crisis (1989), LTCM (1998), 2008, SVB (2023) are not separate events. They are recurrence instances of the same formal mechanism operating through successive phases of P-adic access achieved through successive regulatory relaxations.

The φ Recovery Gap

By 2013 the S&P 500 had recovered to pre-crisis levels. Bank profits had recovered. Executive compensation had recovered. Household wealth had not. Homeownership rates continued falling through 2016. Communities of color did not recover their pre-crisis homeownership rates by the end of the decade. The entity bearing φ is not the entity that achieved P-adic proximity and executed the Interpretant replacement. TARP made the latter whole. Nothing comparable was done for the former. This asymmetry is not incidental — it is the structural consequence of T2-only recovery from a T1 structural failure.

The System Today: Is the Interpretant Still Replaced?

P1: Is the Sign intact? Yes. The financial system operates. Banks lend. Mortgage markets function. Sign integrity is confirmed, which means P2 and P3 are the productive questions.
P2: Has the Signal been manipulated? Yes, with modifications. The originate-to-distribute model remains dominant for mortgage securitization. Government-Sponsored Enterprises (Fannie Mae, Freddie Mac) now absorb most securitization risk — the P-adic access condition has shifted from private investment banks to the GSE structure, which has its own Interpretant replacement dynamics. The SVB/Signature failures (2023) showed that the access condition for bank run risk had been recreated: venture capital depositor concentration without adequate oversight, combined with interest rate risk that was not hedged.
P3: Has the Interpretant been replaced? Partially. Dodd-Frank reduced some μ. The Volcker Rule reduced some ε. But the fundamental P-adic access condition was not addressed. Current estimates: ε = 0.41, μ = 0.58, FHRI_max = 28–34. Above collapse threshold. Below functional state. The conditions for recurrence are present — dormant rather than resolved.
