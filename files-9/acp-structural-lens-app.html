The same institutional harm looks completely different depending on which operating system you use to perceive it. OS1 analysis produces genuine moral insight — it is not wrong. But it is constitutively perspectival: it produces conclusions that are fully coherent from within a particular PRF cluster and that look entirely different from within a different PRF cluster. OS2 analysis identifies the structural layer beneath all OS1 perceptions — the layer that none of the OS1 accounts, alone, can fully reach.

Synthesis: Four Cross-Sector Findings

These four findings emerge from the sector analyses together — none is visible from any single sector analysis alone.

Finding 1: The Scale-Free Law Confirmed

The same five-parameter IPS formula, applied to four sectors using independent data sources, produces high-extraction FHRI_max values within a 22-point range regardless of sector content. The ACP implication: the enabling conditions for moral agency are suppressed to approximately the same degree by extraction architectures in the 11–33 FHRI_max range regardless of whether those architectures operate in nursing homes, prisons, financial systems, or universities.

Finding 2: Exit-Option Modulator

EXIT-ENABLED (avg 29.5)
High-extraction higher education institutions cluster at FHRI_max 27–33. Students can leave — at enormous personal cost in debt already incurred, but they can leave.
CAPTIVE POPULATION (avg 11.9)
Nursing homes, prisons, 2008 mortgage holders cluster at FHRI_max 11–14. The populations bearing φ cannot exit. The extraction architecture faces no competitive pressure from population exit.

Finding 3: φ Distribution Asymmetry is Systematic

In all four sectors, the entity bearing φ is not the entity that achieved P-adic proximity and executed the Interpretant replacement. PE firms are not the nursing home residents. GEO Group shareholders are not the correctional officers experiencing 43% turnover. Investment banks are not the homeowners in foreclosure. For-profit universities are not the students bearing $42K in debt against $35K earnings. This is not coincidental. Extraction architectures specifically route φ to populations furthest from the P-adic access mechanism — and therefore least capable of addressing it through normal market or political channels.

Finding 4: OS1 Limits Are Real and Consistent

Every OS1 prescription reviewed in this analysis was T2 when T1 was required — not because the prescriptions were wrong, but because they operated within the existing access condition rather than addressing it. Stricter nursing home regulations (T2). Better financial regulations (T2). Performance-based prison contracts (T2). Student outcome disclosure (T2). All are genuine improvements within the existing Interpretant replacement. None changes who is inside the channel controlling the Interpretant assignment.
