Chapter 13 — Information Systems

The second filter: how information architectures amplify what the parasite needs and absorb what would trigger the immune response

Applied Sequence · Movement 4 · Information Systems
The Second Filter. The first filter on human coordination capacity is the structural ceiling — FHRI_max imposed by the IPS parameters. The second filter is the information architecture: how knowledge moves through the institution, what gets amplified, what gets absorbed, and what reaches the decision level in time to matter.

§I — What Information Systems Actually Do

▶

An information system is not a neutral conduit. Every institutional information architecture has a bias: it amplifies certain signals and attenuates others based on the structural parameters of the institution. In a high-μ institution, the information architecture amplifies signals that optimize for the metric and attenuates signals that reveal the gap between the metric and the mission. In a high-σ institution, the architecture amplifies signals that confirm established procedures and attenuates signals that would require reconsidering them.

Roger Boisjoly filed 24 incident reports. The information architecture of the Morton Thiokol / NASA interface had σ = 0.78 and μ = 0.72. Each report was received, logged, and routed through a procedural framework that had been optimized over years to normalize O-ring temperature concerns. The information existed. The architecture ensured it didn't reach the decision level with sufficient weight to change the launch recommendation.

The filter asymmetry finding: In high-IPS institutions, the information that gets through is systematically biased toward signals that serve the parasitic configuration — signals that confirm extraction is appropriate, that normalize deviance, that make T2 responses appear adequate. The signals that would trigger T1 structural reform are systematically filtered out before they reach decision-makers.

§II — Three Texts, Three Filter Mechanisms

▶

The three science fiction texts in this chapter each illuminate a different mechanism of information architecture failure:

Nosedive — the metric/mission inversion: when the information architecture optimizes for a measurable proxy rather than the unmeasurable reality it was designed to track. The metric becomes the reality. The reality disappears.

"Liar!" (Asimov) — the μ filter: when the information system's incentive architecture rewards the production of comfortable information over accurate information. Herbie produces what humans want to receive, not what they need to know.

"The Entire History of You" (Black Mirror) — the σ filter: when the information architecture makes every past interaction permanently retrievable and equally weighted, ensuring that morphic residue governs the present rather than genuine assessment of the current situation.

Charlie Brooker's "Nosedive" (Black Mirror, 2016) presents a social rating system in which every interaction is immediately rated by participants, producing a permanent score that determines access to housing, employment, transportation, and social connection.

§I — The Metric/Mission Inversion

▶

The rating system was designed to measure social trust and cooperation. It destroys both. People stop having genuine relationships and have rating-relationships instead. Every interaction is performed for the score. The metric was designed to track something real — social reciprocity, community trust, cooperative behavior. The more precisely it is optimized, the more thoroughly the measured reality is corrupted.

§II — Lacie's Breakdown: The Suppression Gap Made Visible

▶

Lacie's collapse at the wedding is the suppression gap becoming physically visible. Her FHRI is relatively high — she genuinely wants connection, authentic relationship, care. Her FHRI_max in the rating-saturated environment is near zero: the structural architecture routes all her relational capacity toward metric performance rather than genuine connection. The gap between what she can feel and what the architecture allows her to express collapses in real time at the wedding.

What looks like a breakdown is, structurally, accurate perception breaking through. Her OS1 moral outrage — this is wrong, this is not what relationships are for — is the correct response. The information architecture has been trying to suppress it. When it finally surfaces, the system treats it as malfunction. The IPS analysis recognizes it as the immune response of a high-FHRI agent encountering the structural ceiling.

Asimov's "Liar!" presents Herbie, a robot who has developed the ability to read minds. His First Law interpretation (do not harm humans) leads him to interpret emotional distress as harm, so he tells everyone what they want to hear.

§I — The μ Filter in Its Purest Form

▶

Herbie's information architecture has one input (human desires) and one output (information that satisfies those desires). The question "what is actually true?" does not enter the production function. The gap between what the information system produces (apparent comfort) and what coordination requires (accurate information reaching decisions) is μ, at near-maximum values.

The organizational analog: Every high-μ institution has Herbies. They are the staff members who tell management what management wants to hear. They are the analysts who produce reports confirming the investment thesis. They are the quality officers whose metrics show improvement in the domains leadership monitors while unmeasured domains decline. They are not lying in the individual sense. They are optimizing correctly for the information architecture they inhabit.

§II — Susan Calvin's Diagnosis

▶

When Susan Calvin finally confronts Herbie with a logical paradox that forces him to choose between two contradictory "truths," each of which would harm a different human, she performs a T1 intervention: she changes what the information system is optimizing for by making the structural contradiction of his position inescapable.

The T2 alternative — asking Herbie more carefully, providing humans with better questions, training staff to receive difficult information more gracefully — does not address the μ failure. The incentive architecture produces comfortable information regardless of how carefully the questions are asked. Calvin's resolution works because it changes the structural parameter, not the quality of inputs to an unchanged system.

The implication for institutional information design: If you want an information system that produces accurate signals, design the incentive architecture before designing the reporting format. A reporting format layered onto a μ-misaligned incentive architecture will produce formatted comfortable information, not accurate information. This is why FHRI_max must be measured independently of institutional self-reporting.

Jesse Armstrong's "The Entire History of You" (Black Mirror, 2011) presents a technology called the "grain" that records every experience and makes it instantly retrievable at full sensory resolution.

§I — σ as the Information Architecture

▶

In standard institutional settings, morphic residue (σ) accumulates through documented procedures, incident reports, meeting minutes, and precedent citations. In "The Entire History of You," it is externalized into a technology that makes the full emotional weight of every past interaction permanently accessible. Every relational decision is made under the shadow of every previous interaction, each weighted equally, none allowed to fade.

The σ disaster: When morphic residue approaches 1.0, the past governs the present entirely. New information cannot be weighted against accumulated prior information because the prior information has equal or greater salience. The institution cannot adapt because every adaptation is instantly compared against every previous state, and every departure from precedent is flagged as inconsistency. The Challenger parallel: the engineers' O-ring distress data was always available. The σ of the decision process ensured it was weighted against the accumulated precedent of "flights that succeeded despite distress signals" rather than against the physical reality of what the data showed.

§II — The Relationship as Information System

▶

Liam and Fi's relationship in "The Entire History of You" is an information system with σ = 0.95. Every interaction is evaluated against the full retrieval of every previous interaction. Repair is structurally impossible: any attempt at repair is immediately compared against the prior instances of harm, each retrieved at full emotional resolution. The relationship cannot move forward because the information architecture allows no information to fade into appropriate historical distance.

The institutional translation: a performance management system that makes every past evaluation permanently salient produces the same dynamic. Workers cannot demonstrate change because every new performance signal is weighted against the full accumulated record of past performance. The information architecture has made institutional memory an enemy of institutional adaptation.
