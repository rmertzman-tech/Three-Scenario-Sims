The Formally Derived Responsible Party

P-SAGE changes what “responsible party” means. Standard analysis: “global competition and technological change made manufacturing uneconomic.” P-SAGE analysis: “specific actors achieved P-adic proximity to the capital allocation channel through the LBO instrument and executed Interpretant replacement from productive investment to extraction vehicle.” These are different descriptions with different implications for what justice requires.
NOT THE RESPONSIBLE PARTY
Plant managers implementing layoffs
Workers who could not save their jobs
Municipal governments that lost the tax base
Global competition as an abstract force
Technological change as inevitable progress
Chinese manufacturing as a sufficient explanation
IS THE RESPONSIBLE PARTY
The ownership architectures that executed Interpretant replacement (LBO structures, junk bond financing, asset-stripping instruments)

The financial actors who achieved P-adic proximity to the capital allocation channel through acquisition

The regulatory framework that permitted pension obligation defaults, environmental remediation abandonment, and LLC-mediated accountability separation
Structural responsibility vs. individual culpability. The PE partner who structured the LBO may not have understood the FHRI_max implications for the community. Before the IPS measurement existed, structural responsibility existed without full individual culpability. After the IPS measurement exists and FHRI_max trajectories are citable, any ownership architecture that imposes an FHRI_max trajectory from 52 to 11 on a community is making a choice whose consequences are known. That is individual culpability layered on structural responsibility. The measurement changes the moral equation.
The political danger and why the framing is necessary anyway. “The extraction architecture” risks being too abstract to motivate action. “The LBO financiers destroyed our community” risks producing witch-hunts rather than structural reform. The P-SAGE path: name the mechanism (P-adic proximity + Interpretant replacement), name the instrument that achieved it (LBO acquisition), name the structural actors who deployed it, and direct the moral outrage toward the structural change that would prevent it from recurring — not toward punishment of historical actors but toward T1 reform of the regulatory conditions that made it possible and still permit analogous structures today.

What T1 Reform Would Have Required

📋
Pension obligation protection T1
Prohibiting LBO structures that place pension obligations junior to acquisition debt in bankruptcy proceedings. This addresses the P-adic access condition directly: it changes what the acquiring financial actor is legally permitted to do with the capital allocation channel. Currently, pension default is a standard LBO instrument. This is a legal design choice, not an economic necessity.
📊
Ownership disclosure and FHRI_max floor T1
Requiring beneficial ownership disclosure for firms above a threshold employment level makes the P-adic access mechanism visible. Requiring FHRI_max maintenance above a floor as a condition of operating in publicly supported markets (Medicare, Medicaid, federal contracts, public pension investment) changes what the channel is permitted to produce.
💸
Patient capital incentives T1
Tax structures favoring long-horizon productive capital over short-horizon financial engineering change the P-adic access condition at the market level: they make extraction-oriented LBO structures less profitable relative to productive investment structures. This is a tax policy choice that has been repeatedly available and repeatedly not made. It is not an economic necessity that short-horizon capital dominates.
