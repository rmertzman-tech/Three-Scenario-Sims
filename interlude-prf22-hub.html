What Is a Digital E-Twin?

▶
E-Twin = Ethics Module Twin

A parameterized model of your ethical perception and reasoning. Built from 22 measured parameters across three domains. Exported as a JSON file that personalizes every subsequent app's feedback to your specific moral topology.

The JSON File is Your Key

Every remaining app in the ecosystem accepts this file and uses it to personalize its output. Keep it saved. When the course simulations show a suppression gap for agents with your foundation configuration, the E-Twin is how you know those agents are like you.

Privacy: Your PRF-22 data never leaves your device. Nothing is uploaded. No one — including your instructor — can see your individual responses unless you choose to share the file. The apps run entirely in your browser.

Understanding the E-Twin's limits is as important as understanding its capabilities. These are not flaws in the instrument. They are the boundary conditions that make structural analysis in Chapters 6–14 necessary even for students who know their PRF precisely.
🧠 It cannot tell you what you will do under actual stress.
The PRF-22 measures your values in a low-stakes reflective context — Manual Mode responses. Under genuine stress, organizational authority, or peer pressure, your behavior will be influenced by automatic-mode processing that may diverge substantially from your PRF profile. Roger Boisjoly's E-Twin would have shown high professional integrity, high Care for safety. His behavior confirmed this. But the organizational structure tested whether those weights could sustain Manual Mode under sustained assault. The E-Twin shows what you value. The simulations in subsequent chapters show what happens when the institutional environment makes acting on those values costly.
⚖️ It cannot tell you which ethical judgments are correct.
The PRF-22 is a descriptive instrument, not a normative one. It tells you how ethical situations feel to you — which aspects you prioritize, which you find obvious, which you find difficult. It does not tell you which responses are morally justified. Two people with very different PRF configurations can both be making morally serious, defensible judgments. SAGE provides the normative resources the descriptive PRF does not supply. Your E-Twin shows where you stand. The traditions show the full landscape of where principled people can stand and why.
🏛️ It cannot tell you about structural constraints on acting on your values.
The most important analytical insight in this course is that the gap between what people value and what they can enact is primarily a structural problem, not a character problem. Healthcare workers who score high on Care and then find themselves unable to provide the care they believe in are not failing their values. They are operating in an institutional environment with a suppression gap between what agents know is right and what the architecture allows to reach decisions. Your E-Twin describes your values. It cannot tell you about the suppression gap you will encounter when you try to act on them. Chapters 6–14 show what that gap looks like in real institutional environments.
🕸️ It cannot tell you how your PRF interacts with others' in complex systems.
Chapter 4's agent-based modeling showed that individual preferences, even moderate ones, can produce large-scale collective patterns that no individual intends or foresaw. Your PRF interacts with every other PRF in any institutional environment you inhabit. The emergent pattern of those interactions is what the simulation engine models. Understanding your own PRF is necessary but not sufficient for understanding what you will encounter in real institutions. That is why the simulations matter — they show what happens when many different PRF configurations interact under the specific institutional conditions that the evidence architecture and trust vector matrix define.
The E-Twin as a starting point. Everything the PRF-22 ecosystem has shown you in this Interlude is the foundation for the analytical work in Chapters 6–14. Your E-Twin is not a ceiling. It is a map of where you are standing when the applied work begins. The four limits above are not reasons to distrust it — they are the reasons why the structural analysis in the chapters ahead is necessary alongside the self-knowledge this Interlude provides.
