λ Raw
Coordination Capacity
The group's potential — what it could achieve if nothing were blocking it
🧠 What it feels like — Phenomenological layer

When λ Raw is high, you are in what the model calls Manual mode — you are thinking clearly, deliberately, with your full cognitive repertoire available. You haven't lost the thread of who you are in this institution. You can hold complexity, sit with ambiguity, consider multiple angles before acting. You feel, in a word, present — genuinely here, capable of exercising the kind of ethical reasoning that the situation requires.

Your mind is working the way it's supposed to. You can feel the full weight of the problem, hold competing considerations at once, and still act from something that feels like your actual values rather than just institutional reflex.

When λ Raw is falling, something shifts. It isn't dramatic at first — you're still functioning, still showing up, still saying the right things. But the thinking gets flatter. The complexity starts to feel like noise. The ethical nuances that used to feel important start to feel like luxuries you can't afford right now. You are moving toward what the model calls Automatic mode — reactive, pattern-matching, self-protective.

You're not confused. You're not lazy. You've just learned, through accumulated stress, to protect yourself first. The careful, complex thinking hasn't disappeared — it's just become too expensive to access when the environment is this hostile.

When λ Raw collapses to survival mode, the whole calculation changes. You are no longer thinking about the problem. You are thinking about your position relative to the problem. Ethical reasoning requires enough safety and stability to afford deliberation. When that safety is gone, survival takes over — not because you lack integrity, but because the institution has made integrity structurally too costly to maintain.

The OS2 Connection

OS2 capabilities (per-worldview coordination skills: mutual_support, role_scaffolding, stewardship_practice, etc.) do not enter λ Raw directly. They operate through r_repair via the BioBond bonus term (20% weight) and through the OS2 synergy score: synergy = 0.70 × mean(coordination_readiness × C_PFC) + 0.30 × capability_diversity. When BioBond is activated at step 4, it injects this synergy as a bonus that reduces stress accumulation and boosts repair capacity simultaneously.

What this means: OS2 capability amplifies the repair channel. Agents with high coordination_readiness and high C_PFC get the most benefit from OS2 scaffolding. Agents already in survival mode (C_PFC ≈ 0) get almost no benefit — because the BioBond bonus must be multiplied by C_PFC before it has any effect. The intervention must arrive before the collapse, not after.

The PRF Kernel Truncation Mechanism

Under high stress (L_GC → 1.0), the metabolic budget available for identity maintenance shrinks:

thermal_load = 1 + 20 × max(0.1, L_GC) max_depth = floor( 4.0 / (0.05 × thermal_load) ) # At L_GC = 0.65 → max_depth ≈ 5 of possible 20 # At L_GC = 0.90 → max_depth ≈ 3 (survival threshold) prf_kernel.truncate(max_depth) # Loses access to deeper reasoning layers h_cont = active_depth / total_depth # Falls as kernel shrinks

Pedagogical significance: This is the institutional memory mechanism. When individuals or populations are under sustained stress, they don't just reason worse — they lose access to the depth of their reasoning history. The accumulated PRF (who I am, what I've learned, what my values ground me in) becomes inaccessible. The generational simulation shows whether institutions are transmitting or truncating this depth.

λ Perceived
Experienced Coordination
What it actually feels like to try to coordinate — after the system has filtered what you're allowed to see and say
🎭 What it feels like — Phenomenological layer

λ Perceived is what coordination feels like from the inside — after the institutional filter has done its work. It is always equal to or lower than λ Raw. When the two are close, you are operating close to your actual capacity. When they diverge, you are performing coordination at a fraction of what you could do if the system weren't filtering what you're allowed to contribute.

The difference between λ Raw and λ Perceived is the gap between what you know and what you can safely say. You have the knowledge. The system just makes it costly, dangerous, or structurally impossible to use it.

The mechanism is evidence policy distortion — each agent's evidence policy filters and biases the observation vector before coordination happens. An agent running on authority-weighted evidence treats authoritative sources as more reliable than empirical observation. One running on alternative facts introduces noise into every perception. The result: agents are making decisions about how to coordinate based on a distorted picture of reality, even when their raw capacity to coordinate is high.

The Suppression Gap — λ Raw minus λ Perceived — is the most important derived quantity in the model. A large gap means the system has significant untapped intelligence. Decisions are being made without access to what the people in the room actually know. This is the Boisjoly number: the gap between the engineering knowledge that existed and the knowledge that could reach the decision.

You have something important to say. But the room has a structure to it — who speaks first, who's already made up their mind, what it would cost you to contradict them — and that structure makes saying the true thing feel riskier than saying the safe thing. So you say something adjacent. Softer. Framed as a question. It doesn't land.
Gap SizeSuppression LevelWhat's Happening
< 5% Minimal — agents using full voice What people know is reaching decisions. Rare in real institutions.
5 – 15% Mild — some self-censorship Concerns surface carefully, hedged and routed through approved channels.
15 – 30% Significant — room knows more than it says Consequential knowledge is not reaching decisions. Institutional harm becomes likely.
> 30% Critical — decisions without full intelligence The Boisjoly pattern. Multiple framework readings apply — see below.
How λ Perceived Is Computed

λ Perceived uses exactly the same formula as λ Raw, but applied to perceived_obs rather than obs:

perceived_obs = apply_evidence_policy( obs, evidence_policy ) λ_Perceived = compute_lambda( perceived_obs )
The Evidence Policy Mechanism

Each agent has an evidence policy that distorts its observation vector before coordination happens. Four policies are implemented:

PolicyMechanismEffect on λ Perceived
empirical_calibratedNo distortion, no masking, no noiseλ Perceived ≈ λ Raw — near-full intelligence surfacing
authority_weightedBiases toward authority-confirming features; masks community signalsSuppresses relational and repair dimensions; amplifies role conformity
tradition_weightedBiases toward conservation; masks openness and repair signalsSuppresses adaptive capacity; amplifies historical continuity
alternative_factsHigh disconfirmation_resistance + field_noise injectionLargest λ Perceived suppression; agents coordinating on distorted reality
The Suppression Gap Formula
gap = λ_Raw − λ_Perceived = compute_lambda(obs) − compute_lambda(perceived_obs) # The gap is entirely explained by evidence policy distortion. # If all agents ran empirical_calibrated evidence policy: # perceived_obs ≈ obs → gap ≈ 0 # The gap is not a capacity deficit — it is an access deficit.

Key insight: λ Raw measures what the group could achieve if information flowed freely. λ Perceived measures what actually flows through the system's power and evidence structure. The gap between them is the cost of information distortion — which is what suppression, hierarchy, and evidence filtering actually produce in coordination terms. It is a measurable quantity, not a judgment.

Friction
Coordination Cost
The energy the system consumes just to function — before any of it reaches the actual problem
⚡ What it feels like — Phenomenological layer

Friction is the tax you pay just to work together — before you've done anything useful. It is not the same as the suppression gap. Friction is about worldview distance: how much translation work is required before agents who reason differently can actually coordinate. A room with high friction isn't necessarily a room where people are being silenced. It might be a room where everyone is speaking honestly but every sentence requires negotiation before it can be received.

The meeting is honest. No one is being silenced. But it still takes three times as long as it should. You're not navigating deception — you're navigating the distance between how you each understand what the problem even is.

High friction and high suppression are different problems requiring different solutions. Friction is a diversity challenge: people are genuinely far apart in their moral frameworks, and bridging that distance costs coordination energy. The solution is better OS2 scaffolding — structured processes that help different frameworks work together without requiring them to merge. Suppression is a power challenge: people are being prevented from contributing what they know. The solution is structural safety — protected channels for dissent.

Confusing these two is expensive. If your problem is suppression and you add more process structure, you've made it harder to speak. If your problem is friction and you increase psychological safety, you've still got agents who can't understand each other's moral vocabulary. Diagnosis precedes prescription.
ValueDrag LevelWhat This Costs
< 0.25 Low drag Most coordination energy reaches the actual problem. Frameworks are close or well-bridged.
0.25 – 0.45 Moderate drag Noticeable translation cost. Coordination is possible but taxing over time.
0.45 – 0.65 High drag Significant energy consumed internally before any work on the problem begins.
> 0.65 Severe drag System is consuming itself. Most energy goes to internal resistance. Not sustainable.
Friction Formula (Platform Level)
friction = mean( _pair_friction(agent_i, agent_j) for sampled pairs (i, j) ) _pair_friction(a, b) = 0.5 × worldview_distance(a, b) + 0.5 × evidence_distance(a, b) − os2_synergy_discount worldview_distance = Jensen-Shannon divergence of block weight distributions evidence_distance = cosine distance of source weight vectors
OS2 Synergy Discount
os2_synergy = 0.70 × mean( a.os2.coordination_readiness × a.C_PFC ) + 0.30 × capability_diversity # capability_diversity = unique top capabilities / total possible # High diversity means different frameworks contributing complementary skills # This is the mechanism by which pluralism reduces friction rather than increasing it

The crucial insight: OS2 capability diversity reduces friction when it is present alongside high C_PFC. A room with five different frameworks and high C_PFC in each agent can be lower-friction than a homogeneous room — because each framework brings a complementary coordination skill. This is the empirical case for pluralism in institutional design, expressed as a computable quantity.

But the same diversity at low C_PFC — survival mode — produces high friction without the synergy discount, because the coordination_readiness term is multiplied by C_PFC. Diversity only reduces friction when the agents have enough capacity to actually use their distinctive capabilities. This is why institutional stress is doubly destructive: it collapses capacity and eliminates the synergy benefit of diversity.

C_PFC
The Gate — Manual Mode and the Meta-Constructor
The variable everything depends on: deliberate reasoning capacity, and when it collapses
🔑 What it feels like — Phenomenological layer

C_PFC is the prefrontal control capacity — the engine variable that determines whether an agent is in Manual mode (deliberate, reflective, capable of ethical reasoning) or Automatic mode (reactive, pattern-driven, self-protective). It is the gate through which all seven λ Raw modifiers must pass. When C_PFC is high, the full coordination capacity of the agent is available. When C_PFC collapses, nothing else matters — the formula becomes: λ Raw ≈ 0 regardless of how good the relationships are or how clear the thinking was yesterday.

Manual mode is when you can actually think. You're not just running your habitual patterns — you're genuinely deliberating. You can catch yourself mid-reflex and ask whether this is actually the right thing to do. This is where ethical reasoning lives. Automatic mode isn't stupidity. It's what happens when the system has made deliberation too expensive to afford.

C_PFC is driven by glucocorticoid load (L_GC) — the model's measure of accumulated biological stress. When L_GC rises above the critical threshold (θ_crit = 0.65), C_PFC collapses via a steep sigmoid function. The transition is not gradual — it is a threshold effect. Below the threshold, you're managing. Above it, the switch flips and deliberative reasoning becomes largely inaccessible.

This is also the connection to OS1 and OS2 reasoning in the UCP model. OS1 is the interpretive framework — the belief/worldview layer that filters perception. OS2 is the capability layer — the coordination skills that allow agents to work together across difference. Both require C_PFC to function. OS2 coordination_readiness is multiplied by C_PFC in every calculation — which means that no amount of OS2 capability in the population matters if the institution has driven C_PFC below threshold.

This is the cruelest thing institutional stress does: it dismantles the exact capacity you need to recognize that the institution is damaging you. The thinking that would let you see the problem clearly is the thinking the stress has taken away.

The generational simulation shows something important about what populations that maintain high λ Raw across generations have preserved. They haven't just preserved coordination skill — they have preserved the conditions for deliberate reasoning itself. They have kept C_PFC above threshold, kept the PRF kernel intact, kept the repair channels open. A population that does this across time is one whose institutional culture has found ways to protect the very capacity for ethical thought — not just to exercise it in the present, but to transmit the conditions that make it possible to the next generation.

The C_PFC Sigmoid Gate
# Stress accumulation (exponential moving average) current_stress = max(0, scenario.base_entropy + d_morph − biobond_bonus) L_GC = 0.8 × L_GC + 0.2 × current_stress # C_PFC via logistic gate (K=15, θ=0.65) exponent = 15.0 × (L_GC − 0.65) C_PFC = 1 / (1 + exp(exponent)) # Threshold behavior: # L_GC = 0.50 → C_PFC ≈ 0.90 (well above — full manual mode) # L_GC = 0.65 → C_PFC = 0.50 (critical threshold — mode switch) # L_GC = 0.80 → C_PFC ≈ 0.10 (near collapse) # L_GC = 0.90 → C_PFC ≈ 0.02 (effectively zero)
OS1 Mode Determination
os1.mode = "MANUAL" if C_PFC > 0.50 = "AUTOMATIC" otherwise # MANUAL mode: full deliberative reasoning available # → OS2 capabilities active at full coordination_readiness × C_PFC # → PRF kernel can expand (accretion > ablation when stress is moderate) # → Ethical complexity is navigable # AUTOMATIC mode: reactive, pattern-driven # → OS2 synergy discount ≈ 0 (C_PFC near zero) # → PRF kernel truncating (ablation > accretion) # → Agent prioritizes self-protection over coordination
The Growth vs. Ablation Competition
# PRF Kernel can expand (learning) or contract (stress truncation) accretion = 0.10 × C_PFC × phi # phi = neighbor diversity signal ablation = GAMMA_DECAY × entropy × exp(BETA_STRESS × L_GC) delta_L = accretion − ablation # When C_PFC is high AND stress is moderate: # accretion > ablation → PRF kernel expands → h_cont increases → λ Raw ↑ # (The institution is building reasoning depth) # When L_GC is high: # ablation dominates → PRF kernel truncates → h_cont falls → λ Raw ↓ # (The institution is eroding reasoning depth faster than it's built)
Why Population-Level λ Raw Matters for OS2

At the population level, OS2 synergy is:

os2_synergy = 0.70 × mean( a.os2.coordination_readiness × a.C_PFC for all agents ) + 0.30 × capability_diversity

Key implication: A population with high OS2 capability scores but low C_PFC produces near-zero OS2 synergy — because every capability is multiplied by C_PFC before it counts. The engine is explicit that capability without capacity is inert. This is the formal statement of your intuition: maintaining high λ Raw across generations is maintaining the population-level C_PFC that makes OS2 reasoning actually available. Without it, all the coordination skills in the world remain unused.

The generational simulation makes this visible: under Top-λ selection, if the scenario's stress conditions are driving L_GC above threshold, the simulation can select for agents who coordinate well despite low C_PFC — agents who have learned to navigate the system in Automatic mode. These agents produce good short-term coordination metrics but transmit a population that is progressively less capable of the deliberate ethical reasoning that Manual mode enables. High λ Raw in Generation 0, falling h_cont and rising v_surv by Generation 4: the institution selected for survivors, not reasoners.

CIB ⟳
The Full Loop — Coherent Identity Blueprint
How identity, stress, capacity, and coordination form a closed system
🔄 The Loop in Plain Language

The CIB loop is the engine's model of how identity, stress, and coordination capacity interact across time. It is a closed system with two competing dynamics:

The Stress Loop (downward spiral): When who you believe you are (PRF kernel) diverges from what the institution is asking you to be (ITA kernel), the distance between them — d_morph — accumulates as stress (L_GC). Enough accumulated stress collapses C_PFC. Collapsed C_PFC means the repair channels close, learning stops, and the PRF kernel starts to truncate. A truncated PRF kernel deepens the distance from the ITA kernel, generating more stress. The loop feeds itself.

The Growth Loop (upward spiral): When L_GC is below the critical threshold — when the institution provides enough safety and alignment — C_PFC stays high. High C_PFC means repair is available, learning can happen, and the PRF kernel can expand. An expanding PRF kernel reduces d_morph (the agent grows into better alignment with its role without losing its identity), which reduces stress, which keeps C_PFC high. This is the virtuous cycle.

OS2 and BioBond as circuit-breakers: OS2 coordination scaffolding and BioBond interventions enter the loop as a biobond_bonus that reduces current_stress before it accumulates into L_GC. This is why they work: they interrupt the stress loop at the source rather than trying to restore C_PFC after it has already collapsed. The engine is explicit that agents already in survival mode receive almost no benefit from the intervention — because the bonus is multiplied by C_PFC before it reaches r_repair.

Per-Step Execution Order

Each time-step executes in this exact sequence:

1. d_morph = prf_kernel.metric_distance(ita_kernel) # Identity vs. role divergence 2. current_stress = max(0, entropy + d_morph − biobond_bonus) L_GC = 0.8×L_GC + 0.2×current_stress # Stress EMA 3. C_PFC = sigmoid(−15 × (L_GC − 0.65)) # Gate max_depth = metabolic_budget / thermal_load prf_kernel.truncate(max_depth) # Memory constraint is_survival = (prf_kernel.active_depth < 3) 4. raw_J = build_coupling_matrix() # From worldview block weights raw_h = build_raw_field() # From scenario + d_morph perceived_h = distort_field(raw_h) # Via evidence policy spin_core.anneal(raw_J, perceived_h) # Find energy minimum 5. _update_growth(neighbors, entropy) # accretion vs. ablation 6. _update_observables() # Build obs vector perceived_obs = apply_evidence_policy(obs) 7. λ_Raw = compute_lambda(obs) λ_Perceived = compute_lambda(perceived_obs) Suppression_Gap = λ_Raw − λ_Perceived
The Two Attractors

The loop has two stable attractors — states the system tends toward once perturbed in either direction:

High-C_PFC attractor: L_GC < θ_crit → C_PFC high → accretion > ablation → PRF expands → d_morph stable or falling → less stress → L_GC stays low → C_PFC stays high. Generates high λ Raw, low friction, shrinking suppression gap over time.

Survival attractor: L_GC > θ_crit → C_PFC collapses → ablation > accretion → PRF truncates → h_cont falls → λ Raw falls → survival mode → v_surv = 1.0 → λ Raw ≈ 0. Self-reinforcing. BioBond intervention cannot reach agents already in this attractor because the bonus is multiplied by near-zero C_PFC.

Generational implication: Top-λ selection preserves agents closer to the high-C_PFC attractor — but under severe stress, even high-C_PFC agents drift toward survival mode over generations. The simulation shows whether the scenario's base_entropy is high enough to overwhelm the growth loop. If it is, every generation ends slightly closer to the survival attractor, and the institution's reasoning capacity erodes across time regardless of who is selected.

Each app can be embedded directly in a Canvas page using an iframe, or linked as an external tool. The simplest approach for each app:

  1. In Canvas, create a new Page for each simulation app.
  2. Switch the editor to HTML view (the <> button).
  3. Paste the iframe embed code below, substituting the correct URL.
  4. Set the iframe height to at least 900px — the apps are tall on first run.
  5. To link to this reference page from within an app page, add a standard HTML anchor: <a href="URL" target="_blank">Open Metrics Reference</a>
